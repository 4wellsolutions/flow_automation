Scene 1 — The Beast Awakens

Camera Style: An extreme close-up, shaky-cam shot. The camera starts focused on a single, glowing yellow-orange eye, then pulls back just enough to frame the entire snarling maw of the creature. The shot is at a slight Dutch angle to create a sense of disorientation. The camera vibrates, as if from the force of the roar.

Character: A giant, bipedal, hyper-muscular white were-tiger. This close-up should highlight the intricate, photorealistic textures: matted white and black fur, flecks of saliva and spittle flying from its mouth, long, sharp fangs, and flared nostrils. Its eyes are not animalistic; they are supernatural, glowing with an internal, fiery yellow light.

Environment: A bright, slightly overexposed industrial setting. The background is completely out of focus (a very shallow depth of field), hinting at metal scaffolding and harsh, direct sunlight. The focus is 100% on the creature's face.

Action: The were-tiger is in the middle of a continuous, violent roar. Its entire head, neck, and shoulders strain with the effort, muscles bulging. Its head shakes from side to side in pure, uncontrollable rage.

Mood/Expression: Primal, uncontrollable fury. This is not just an animal; it's a monster of pure rage.

Audio (Sound Design): A deafening, guttural, lion-like roar layered with a deep, human-like scream. Heavy bass that shakes the scene. The high-pitched clang of a heavy metal chain being snapped taut.

Style of Video: Cinematic, photorealistic, high-budget action, CGI creature feature.
