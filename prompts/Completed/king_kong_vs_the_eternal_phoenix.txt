Scene 1: A research chopper flies over a dormant volcano in Chile; ash begins drifting upward, against the wind.

**Scene Title:** Unnatural Ascent
*   **Camera Style:** Extreme wide shot, helicopter mount, slow circular pan. Utilizing a wide-angle lens to emphasize the vast, desolate scale of the landscape. The camera movement is steady, almost serene, creating a stark contrast with the unnatural event about to occur.
*   **Environment:** The jagged caldera of a dormant Chilean volcano, dusted with patches of old snow and grey pumice. The sky is a harsh, crystal-clear blue. The lighting is the unforgiving glare of high-altitude sun. The air is thin and cold.
*   **Character & Action:** A red and white research helicopter cuts across the frame, its rotors kicking up minimal dust. Inside, a scientist points a sensor out the window. Suddenly, from the volcano's crater, fine grey ash begins to rise, not billowing, but drifting gently *upward*, moving against the prevailing wind in delicate, eerie tendrils.
*   **Mood/Expression:** Ominous, unsettling, a quiet violation of natural laws.
*   **Audio:** The rhythmic *thump-thump-thump* of the helicopter blades dominates, mixed with the high-altitude wind whistle. A low, almost subsonic hum begins to fade in as the ash rises, a sound that feels more like a pressure change than a noise.
